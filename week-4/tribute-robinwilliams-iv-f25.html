Robin Williams

1951–2014 · Actor & Comedian

Biography Highlights

Robin Williams (1951–2014) was an American actor and comedian known for quick-fire improv and big-hearted performances; from Mork & Mindy and stand-up to Good Will Hunting (Academy Award, 1998), Dead Poets Society, Aladdin, and Mrs. Doubtfire. His range across comedy and drama continues to inspire performers and audiences worldwide.


Timeline

  • 1951 - Born July 21 in Chicago, Illinois.
  • 1960s - Grows up in Michigan and California; develops interest in comedy and acting.
  • 1969 - Graduates from Redwood High School in Larkspur California.
  • 1973 - Accepted into Juilliard School in New York City; studies drama alongside Christopher Reeve.
  • 1976 - Leaves Juilliard to pursue stand-up comedy in San Francisco and Los Angeles.
  • 1978 - Gains national fame as Mork in TV series Mork & Mindy (spin off of Happy Days).
  • 1980 - Makes film debut in Popeye, directed by Robert Altman.
  • 1982-1997 - Appears in a mix of comedies and dramas, including The World According to Garp and Good Morning, Vietnam (Oscar Nomination).
  • 1989 - Stars in Dead Poets Society (Oscar Nomination).
  • 1991 - Plays Peter Banning in Hook and therapist Sean Maguire in Good Will Hunting (later wins an Oscar for this role).
  • 1992 - Voices the Genie in Disney's Aladdin.
  • 1993 - Stars in Mrs. Doubtfire, one of his biggest box-office successes.
  • 1997 - Wins Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for Good Will Hunting.
  • 2000s - Continues acting in diverse roles (One Hour Photo, Insomnia, Night at the Museum).
  • 2009 - Undergoes heart surgery; later returns to comedy and touring.
  • 2011-2013 - Appears in films like The Big Wedding and TV show The Crazy Ones.
  • 2014 - Passes away on August 11 in Tiburon, California at age 63.
  • 2015 - Posthumously revealed to have suffered from Lewy body dementia, which contributed to his mental and physical struggles.

"You're only given a little spark of madness. You mustn't lose it."
- Robin Williams

Context & Legacy

Williams bridged improv comedy and heartfelt storytelling, showing how humor can carry empathy and catharsis. His animated voice work and dramatic roles expanded what comedic actors could do on screen. Tributes, charity, and continuing fan communities reflect an enduring impact.
